05 — What We're Building

The Automation, Step by Step

Every guest communication from booking to checkout, handled automatically. Your team does nothing for any of these to happen.

⚡
Trigger — New Booking in Cloudbeds

A webhook fires the moment a reservation is confirmed on any channel — Booking.com, Airbnb, or your website widget. Make.com receives the full reservation data and validates it immediately. If anything is missing (e.g. no guest email), Maria is alerted instantly with the booking ID.

Real-time trigger Cloudbeds webhook
1
Confirmation Email Sent

Within minutes of the booking, the guest receives a personalised confirmation from your hotel's Gmail — their name, room type, check-in/out dates, rate, breakfast inclusion, cancellation policy, and your direct contact details. Branded. Accurate. Zero typing by Maria.

Sent instantly Via Gmail Personalised template
2
Pre-Arrival Email — Scheduled Automatically

The automation calculates exactly 3 days before check-in and schedules the pre-arrival email to fire at 09:00 Europe/Athens. For last-minute bookings (check-in within 3 days), it sends immediately. No one on your team has to remember, set a reminder, or calendar anything.

Scheduled · D-3 at 09:00 Instant for late bookings
3
Pre-Arrival Email Delivered

The guest receives: directions from Santorini port and airport, parking information, check-in time (15:00), WiFi name and password, breakfast time and location, and an emergency contact number. Everything they need, before they need it. Zero missed sends.

Automatic Zero missed sends
4
PDF Invoice Generated & Emailed on Checkout

On checkout day at 11:00, the automation pulls the final reservation data from Cloudbeds, generates a correctly calculated PDF invoice — hotel logo, 13% VAT, correct dates, correct totals — and emails it to the guest. For corporate clients, the invoice is produced in both English and Greek.

Checkout day · 11:00 PDF attachment EN + GR for corporate
+
Website Inquiries — Instant Acknowledgement

When a potential guest submits your WordPress contact form, they receive an automatic reply within seconds confirming their inquiry was received. Maria gets a formatted summary in her inbox — ready to reply. Every inquiry is logged to a Google Sheet automatically.

Instant auto-reply Maria notified Logged to Google Sheets

Error Handling — Nothing Falls Through the Cracks

⚠
Email fails to send System retries automatically → If retry fails: Maria + Nikos receive an alert with the guest's details and clear manual instructions
⚠
Guest email missing from booking Immediate alert to Maria with booking ID → Maria fixes the booking manually → Flow can be re-triggered
⚠
PDF invoice generation fails Retry once → If still fails: plain-text invoice sent as fallback → Alert Maria to send PDF version manually
⚠
Duplicate booking detected (same booking ID) Automation skips it automatically — no double emails sent to guests under any circumstances

07 — The Road Ahead

Three Follow-On Opportunities

Based on everything we learned, these are the highest-value automation opportunities to pursue once the booking communication flow is live and stable.

Next · 1–2 months after launch
1. Guest Review Request Flow
Complexity
Low

After checkout, most hotels lose the review opportunity because the request arrives too late, too early, or never at all. An automated review request fires 24 hours after checkout — personalised from Nikos, linking directly to Booking.com and Google review pages. A gentle follow-up fires 3 days later for guests who didn't open the first email.

Why now: Uses the exact same checkout-day trigger already deployed. Zero new infrastructure. Industry data shows this increases review volume by 30–50% — directly lifting Booking.com ranking.

Build Time
1–2 weeks
New Tools
None
Impact
+30–50% reviews
Before next summer
2. Cancellation & Rebooking Flow
Complexity
Medium

When a guest cancels, the room sits empty unless someone acts fast. A cancellation in Cloudbeds triggers two flows simultaneously: a recovery email to the cancelled guest offering flexible rebooking, and an internal alert to Nikos with the room, dates, and availability to decide whether to push a last-minute deal.

Why now: A single recovered room night in July or August is worth €200–400. Even a 10% recovery rate pays for this build many times over in the first summer.

Build Time
3–4 weeks
Potential Value
€200–400 / recovered night
Impact
High in peak season
Before second season
3. Seasonal Upsell & Loyalty Flow
Complexity
High

Returning guests are the most valuable and least expensive to acquire — but most hotels treat them identically to first-timers. This flow recognises returning guests automatically, sends pre-season priority booking windows to past guests, and offers room upgrades to incoming guests 2 weeks before arrival.

Why now: The guest data already exists in Cloudbeds. Early-season outreach can fill 10–20% of peak inventory before it hits booking platforms — at zero channel commission (typically 15–18% on Booking.com).
